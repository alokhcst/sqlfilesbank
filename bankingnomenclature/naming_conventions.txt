A good banking data warehouse naming convention combines generic DW best practices (clear, consistent, layer-aware) with domain-specific prefixes and subject areas (customer, accounts, loans, cards, risk, regulatory, etc.). Below is a sample scheme you can adapt, with concrete examples.[1][2]

## Core principles

- Use lowercase, underscores, no spaces or special characters; avoid reserved words.[3][1]
- Prefer clear business terms over cryptic abbreviations; where abbreviations are needed, maintain a shared glossary (e.g., `acct`, `txn`, `kpi`).[4][5]
- Use prefixes (or suffixes) to signal both layer and object type (staging, core DW, marts, dim/fact/vw).[2][1]

## Layers and schemas

Typical schemas for a banking DWH (could be separate DBs or schemas in Snowflake/Redshift/BigQuery):

- `src_*` – raw landing from source systems (core banking, cards, CRM).  
- `stg_*` – staged, minimally cleaned data.  
- `dw_core` – conformed, integrated warehouse (dimensions and facts).  
- `dm_*` – data marts (retail, corporate, risk, finance, regulatory).  

Example schemas:
- `src_corebank`, `src_crm`, `src_cards`  
- `stg_corebank`, `stg_crm`  
- `dw_core`  
- `dm_retail`, `dm_corporate`, `dm_risk`, `dm_finance`, `dm_regulatory`[2]

## Table naming pattern

Use a consistent pattern like:

- Staging: `stg_<source>_<entity>`  
  - `stg_corebank_customer`  
  - `stg_corebank_account`  
  - `stg_cards_auth_txn`  

- Core dimensions: `dim_<subject_area>_<entity>`  
  - `dim_cust_customer` (customer master)  
  - `dim_acct_account` (account master)  
  - `dim_prod_loan_product`  
  - `dim_org_branch`  
  - `dim_gl_gl_account`  

- Core facts: `fct_<subject_area>_<grain>` (grain always explicit).[5]
  - `fct_acct_daily_balance`  
  - `fct_txn_posted_transaction`  
  - `fct_loan_scheduled_cashflow`  
  - `fct_card_authorization`  
  - `fct_risk_exposure_daily`  

- Data marts: prefix by mart then dim/fact:  
  - `dm_retail.dim_retail_customer`  
  - `dm_retail.fct_retail_txn_daily`  
  - `dm_risk.fct_rwa_regulatory`  
  - `dm_finance.fct_pl_actual`  

## Column naming pattern

Best practice is to standardize common attributes across domains. For example:[3][2]

- Surrogate keys: `<entity>_sk`  
  - `customer_sk`, `account_sk`, `branch_sk`  

- Natural/business keys: `<entity>_bk` or explicit term:  
  - `customer_bk` (e.g., CIF), `account_bk` (core banking account number), `card_number`, `loan_contract_number`  

- Descriptions and codes:  
  - `<entity>_name`, `<entity>_desc`, `<entity>_cd`  
  - `branch_cd`, `branch_name`, `product_cd`, `product_name`  

- Standard date/time columns with clear role:  
  - `txn_date`, `posting_date`, `value_date`  
  - `effective_from_dttm`, `effective_to_dttm` (SCD2)  
  - `created_dttm`, `updated_dttm`, `etl_batch_id`  

- Measures numeric:  
  - `principal_amt`, `interest_amt`, `fee_amt`, `balance_amt`, `limit_amt`, `rwa_amt`, `ecl_amt`  

## Banking subject areas (examples)

Use a controlled vocabulary for subject areas to keep names short and consistent.[4][5]

- Customer & party: `cust`, `pty`  
  - `dim_cust_customer`, `dim_pty_counterparty`  
- Accounts & products: `acct`, `prod`, `loan`, `dep`  
  - `dim_acct_account`, `dim_loan_loan_contract`, `dim_prod_deposit_product`  
- Channels & org: `chnl`, `org`, `branch`, `atm`  
  - `dim_chnl_channel`, `dim_org_branch`, `dim_org_cost_center`  
- Transactions: `txn`, `pay`, `card`, `cash`, `trade`  
  - `fct_txn_posted_transaction`, `fct_pay_outbound_payment`, `fct_card_pos_txn`  
- Risk & regulatory: `risk`, `reg`, `rwa`, `liq`, `npa`  
  - `fct_risk_exposure_daily`, `fct_reg_rwa_b3_std`, `fct_reg_lcr_daily`, `fct_risk_npa_status`[6][7]

## Sample star schemas (banking)

### Retail deposits

- `dim_cust_customer`  
  - `customer_sk`, `customer_bk`, `customer_name`, `segment_cd`, `kyc_flag`, `country_cd`  

- `dim_acct_account`  
  - `account_sk`, `account_bk`, `product_sk`, `branch_sk`, `opened_date`, `closed_date`, `account_status_cd`  

- `dim_prod_deposit_product`  
  - `product_sk`, `product_cd`, `product_name`, `product_type_cd`  

- `dim_org_branch`  
  - `branch_sk`, `branch_cd`, `branch_name`, `region_cd`  

- `fct_acct_daily_balance`  
  - `account_sk`, `calendar_date`, `balance_amt`, `accrued_interest_amt`  

### Basel / regulatory

- `dim_reg_reporting_entity`  
- `dim_risk_exposure_class`  
- `fct_reg_rwa_b3_std` (Basel III RWA by exposure class, entity, date)  
- `fct_reg_lcr_daily`  

These align with common financial regulation models and Basel-related data structures without copying vendor IP.[7][6]

## Governance best practices

- Document the convention, examples, and abbreviation glossary in Confluence or similar and enforce in code review and DBT/DDL generators.[1][5][3]
- Apply consistently across tools (Snowflake, Power BI datasets, semantic models) so that table and field names are recognizable end-to-end.[2]

If you share your layers (e.g., Data Vault vs Kimball star) and primary platforms (Snowflake vs on-prem), a tailored convention for your bank’s stack can be drafted.
